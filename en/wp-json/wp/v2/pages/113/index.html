Stance classification task<\/h3>\n

Stance classification task aims at estimating politician's position from politician's utterances.
\nIn PoliInfo2, system participating in the task estimates the stances of political parties from the utterances of the members of the Tokyo Metropolitan Assembly.
\nGiven the Tokyo Metropolitan Assembly, topics (agenda), member's list and political denomination list, and the systems classify their stance into two categories (agree or disagree) for each agenda.<\/p>\n

Dialog summarization task<\/h3>\n

Dialog summarization task aims at summarizing the transcript of local assembly, taking the structure of dialogue into account.
\nIn PoliInfo2, systems participating in this task summarize the transcript based on the dialogue structure, which consists of \u201cMembers' questions\u201d and \u201cGovernor's answer\u201d.
\nGiven the transcript and summary conditions (speaker name and number of summary characters etc), they generate the structured document.<\/p>\n

Entity linking task<\/h3>\n

Entity linking task aims at identifying political terms included in politicians' statements, and is to resolve mention recognition, disambiguation and linking the mention with the knowledge base.
\nIn PoliInfo2, Entity linking is the task of assigning a unique identity of \"legal name\" which is one of the political terms.
\nGiven local assembly member's utterances, and systems extract a mention of \u201claw name\u201d and link the mention with the list of law names or Wikipedia.<\/p>\n
